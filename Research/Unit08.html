Research Proposal Outline

Project Title:

Detecting Medical Misinformation on Twitter Using Machine Learning Approaches: A Multimodal Analysis

Significance/Contribution to the Discipline/Research Problem:

The proliferation of medical misinformation on social media platforms has become a significant public health concern leading to severe health and social consequences. These include the spread of false information, the manipulation of public opinion, and the reduction of trust in institutions (Dar & Hashmy, 2023). This research aims to address this issue by leveraging machine learning techniques to detect and combat medical misinformation effectively.

Research Question:

To understand the effectiveness of machine learning techniques in detecting medical misinformation on Twitter.

Aims and Objectives:

Aim:

To develop and evaluate multimodal machine learning models for detecting medical misinformation on Twitter.

Objectives:

  • Investigate the effectiveness of supervised machine learning methods in identifying medical misinformation.
  • Explore the integration of multimodal data (text, images, videos, audio) for improved detection accuracy.
  • Assess the challenges and opportunities in the field of medical misinformation detection on social media platforms.
  • Develop strategies to mitigate the impact of medical misinformation through automated detection and intervention.
  • Identify common themes and strategies in medical misinformation on Twitter.

Key Literature Related to the Project:

  • Wang, Y. et al. (2019): Literature review highlighting the spread of health-related misinformation.
  • Liang, Y. (2023): Trends in Multimodal Machine Learning: Challenges, and Open Questions.
  • Kamiri, W., & Mariga, P. (2021): Analysis of misinformation content and thematic coding.

Methodology/Development Strategy/Research Design:

The research will adopt a mixed-methods approach, combining mainly quantitative analysis of machine learning algorithms with qualitative analysis of misinformation content on Twitter. While the bulk of machine learning investigation primarily revolves around quantitative methodologies, certain fields, such as Natural Language Processing, may necessitate a qualitative research approach. This allows for further content analysis and thematic coding to identify common themes, linguistic patterns, and strategies used in the dissemination of medical misinformation.

Data Collection:

Twitter data containing medical-related content will be collected using APIs, focusing on diverse media types (text, images, videos, audio) and user behaviours.

Data Preprocessing:

Text preprocessing techniques will be employed to clean and normalise the text data. Image and video data will be processed using computer vision algorithms.

Model Development:

Supervised machine learning algorithms, including classifiers and deep learning models, will be trained on the preprocessed data to detect misinformation.

Evaluation:

The performance of the developed models will be evaluated using standard metrics such as accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 score.

Ethical Considerations:

Ethical approval will be obtained for data collection and analysis. Measures will be taken to ensure user privacy and confidentiality. All user names and other identifiable information will be removed to ensure data anonymity, and no data will be obtained from individuals outside the age range of 18-65.

Risk Assessment:

Risks associated with user data will be mitigated through responsible data handling and algorithm development.

Description of Artefact(s) That Will Be Created (If Applicable):

  • The primary artefact of this research will be the developed machine learning models for detecting medical misinformation on Twitter.
  • Literature review.
  • Research paper containing methodology, results, and discussion of the research.
