    Bar Charts in Excel

    Exercise 9.1

    Area 1 Area 2
    A 15.7 21.1
    B 24.3 33.3
    Other 60.0 45.6
    Total 100 100

    Interpretation

    The table presents the percentage distribution of three categories (A, B, and Other) across two areas (Area 1 and Area 2). Here are the key points: Category A: Area 2 has a higher percentage (21.1%) compared to Area 1 (15.7%). Category B: Similarly, Area 2 also has a higher percentage (33.3%) compared to Area 1 (24.3%). Other Category: Area 1 has a significantly higher percentage (60.0%) compared to Area 2 (45.6%). This indicates that the distribution of categories varies between the two areas, with Area 2 having relatively more of categories A and B, while Area 1 has more in the 'Other' category.

    Histograms in Excel

    Exercise 9.3

    Class Relative Frequency
    -3 0.02
    -1 0.04
    1 0.20
    3 0.30
    5 0.22
    7 0.16
    9 0.04
    11 0.02
    Total 1.00

    Interpretation

    The table provides the relative frequencies of different classes, which can be used to construct a histogram. Here are the key points: The most frequent classes are 3 (30%) and 5 (22%). Class 1 also has a notable frequency (20%). Classes -3 and 11 have the lowest frequencies (2% each), indicating these values are rare. The distribution seems to have a peak around the middle classes (1, 3, 5) and tapers off towards the extreme values (-3, -1, 9, 11). This data would be effectively represented by a histogram to show the distribution of relative frequencies across different classes.
